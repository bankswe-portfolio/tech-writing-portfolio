# Articles & Essays

This section collects short analytical articles on where technical writing is heading as it becomes more tightly integrated with modern development workflows.

I’m interested in recent research and practical experiments around SDKs, APIs, and complex systems where documentation is generated from project artifacts, validated in CI, or shaped by how software is actually built and released. That includes academic papers, industry case studies, and tooling work that proposes concrete mechanisms rather than broad advice.

The goal is to surface ideas about how documentation fits directly into the development cycle and what that means for technical writers working alongside engineering teams today.

## Themes

- **Documentation as infrastructure:** documentation treated as a system artifact adjacent to code, tests, and release workflows
- **AI-assisted documentation:** generation, retrieval, verification, and constrained automation
- **Traceability and correctness:** techniques for keeping documentation aligned with code and other evolving artifacts
- **Architecture and abstraction:** how higher-level technical explanations can be regenerated, grounded, and kept useful over time

## What you’ll find here

These pieces are not case studies or product-doc samples. They are analytical articles and essays that synthesize research, tooling, and workflow ideas at the intersection of:

- AI and technical writing
- docs-as-code and CI-driven documentation
- developer documentation for SDKs, APIs, and complex systems
- documentation engineering as a discipline adjacent to software engineering

## Current entries

- **Paper-driven articles:** short essays that interpret research and tooling work for practitioners
- **Longer essays:** first-party analytical writing on research workflows, AI-assisted knowledge work, and documentation systems
